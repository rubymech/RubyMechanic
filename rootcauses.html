Root Cause #1:

Because of the scarcity of refined gasoline in California, the gas prices have gone up creating a burden of transportation to low income residents.

Root Cause #2:

The inequitable burden of toxic exposures in low-income communities parallels the inequitable accessibility to land, parks, wilderness; environmental amenities, all prolonged results of redlining in Oakland.


Evidence #1
  • Inflation, conflict in Ukraine, and supply problems are causing the high gas prices in the Bay. Although California does not directly import oil from Russia, the absence of Russia as a major exporter increases global demand for oil, and influences California’s gas prices.

    • News of rising gas prices

Evidence #2
  • Lower income residents spend 16% of income on gas compared to the 8% for middle income, and 2% for high income residents.

    • Gas prices stretch family budgets

Evidence #1
  • East Bay Regional Parks cover nearly 125,000 acres of land in Alameda and Contra Costa counties east of San Francisco. It is the largest metropolitan regional public parkland in the United states.

    • East Bay Regional Parks

Evidence #2
  • “On average, neighborhoods that are majority nonwhite enjoy access to 44% less park acreage than majority-white neighborhoods. Low-income communities have access to 42% less park space than high-income neighborhoods.”

    • Oaklandside: The Problem and Potential of Oakland's Parks
