Problem Statement
Need Statement
Many people of color in Oakland face modern segregation from the prolonged effects of redlining and generational poverty. The racialized geography in Oakland traps a lot of POC residents in communities burdened with infrastructure deficits, and perpetuates an “environmental racism framework” that creates barriers to environmental opportunity. The lack of accessible transportation and the price of gas makes supposedly accessible regional parks inaccessible for residents living in the flatlands of the East Bay.
Ironically, the establishment of parks in cities is to make up for the growing industry and the growing service industry, leaving some abandoned sites. But the parkland is so far from the industry central of the East Bay, leaving the clean parks for the people who live in the hills, and the poor air quality and inaccessible roads and transportation to lower income people living in the flatlands.
Residents of the East Bay need equitable access to the parks through accessible public transportation services so individuals/families of any financial situation can access the parks without the burden of paying for transportation.
